a letter from all of us

Dear Ma'am Riz,

You taught us more than English.

You taught us how to speak with confidence — how to find words for the things that felt too big for words.

You corrected our grammar, but somewhere along the way, you also corrected our doubts. You made us feel like our voices were worth hearing.

Even in a short amount of time, you became someone unforgettable. Some people take years to leave a mark. You did it in a semester.

We don't know if you fully understand how much you meant to us. That's why we're writing this. So you know.

Some teachers pass through classrooms.
You passed through our lives.
things we'll never forget

Moments That Stayed

The Voice

"Class, listen." — two words, and the whole room went still. You had this way of making silence feel important.

The Look

When someone gave a completely wrong answer, your expression said everything — without saying anything. We still laugh about it.

The Tangents

Mid-lesson, mid-sentence — you'd pause and share something real. A piece of wisdom dressed up as a side comment. Those were the parts we wrote down.

The Grace

You never made anyone feel stupid. Not once. That's rarer than you think, and it means more than you know.

The Patience

When we were slow, you waited. When we were loud, you smiled. You chose us — every day — even when it wasn't easy.
